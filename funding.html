Funding Opportunities for Startups

Grants

Grants are non-repayable funds provided by organizations, governments, or institutions to help startups grow.

  • Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) - Government-backed grants for tech startups.
  • National Science Foundation (NSF) Grants - Supports research-based innovations.
  • Local Government Grants - Check regional development programs for startup grants.

Loans

Startup loans are financial aids that need to be repaid but can help businesses scale up.

  • SBA Startup Loans - U.S. Small Business Administration-backed loans.
  • Microloans - Small-scale loans for new businesses.
  • Bank & Private Loans - Offered by financial institutions.

Investors

Investors provide funding in exchange for equity or future returns.

  • Angel Investors - Individuals investing in early-stage startups.
  • Venture Capitalists - Firms that provide large-scale investments for high-growth startups.
  • Crowdfunding Platforms - Public funding sources like Kickstarter and Indiegogo.
